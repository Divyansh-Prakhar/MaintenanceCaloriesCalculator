Diet Information

Keto Diet:
The ketogenic (keto) diet is a high-fat, low-carb diet that aims to induce ketosis, a metabolic state where your body burns fat for fuel instead of carbohydrates. Food sources: Meat, fish, eggs, butter, cheese, nuts, seeds, avocados.


High-Carb Diet:
A high-carb diet typically consists of a higher proportion of carbohydrates compared to fats and proteins. Food sources: Whole grains (such as oats, quinoa, brown rice), fruits, vegetables, legumes (beans, lentils), potatoes.


Low-Carb Diet:
A low-carb diet restricts carbohydrate intake, often for weight loss or blood sugar control. Food sources: Non-starchy vegetables (leafy greens, broccoli, cauliflower), meat, fish, eggs, nuts, seeds, some fruits (berries in moderation).


Intermittent Fasting:
Intermittent fasting involves cycling between periods of eating and fasting, with various methods like 16/8, where you fast for 16 hours and eat within an 8-hour window. Food sources: During eating periods, focus on balanced meals with lean protein, healthy fats, and plenty of fruits and vegetables.


High-Protein Foods

Vegetarian Sources:

Lentils: A great source of protein and fiber.
Chickpeas: Versatile legume used in various dishes.
Tofu: Soy-based protein alternative.
Quinoa: High-protein grain with all essential amino acids.
Greek Yogurt: Contains probiotics and is high in protein.
Almonds: Protein-rich nuts with healthy fats.
Edamame: Young soybeans rich in protein and fiber.
Chia Seeds: Packed with protein, fiber, and omega-3 fatty acids.
Non-Vegetarian Sources:

Chicken Breast: Lean source of protein.
Salmon: Rich in omega-3 fatty acids and protein.
Turkey: Lean meat high in protein.
Eggs: Versatile and affordable source of protein.
Tuna: Low in fat and high in protein.
Shrimp: Low in calories and high in protein.
Beef: Provides essential nutrients and protein.
